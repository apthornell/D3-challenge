Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks, Age, and Level of Income

The effects of poverty seem to have a positive correlation on all of these factors. So the lower your income is, the more likely you are to lack healthcare, be a smoker, and be obese.

There does not seem to be a correlation to age with these factors. All the data seems to be in the median, and there is not a correlation to age and the three lifestyle factors.

As to be expected, we see the opposite trend as poverty when looking at Median Household Income. The higher that the income is for the household, the less likely you are to be obese, be a smoker, or lack healthcare.
